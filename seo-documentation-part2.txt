--- HOMEPAGE FAQ (FaqAccordion.tsx + JSON-LD FAQPage schema) ---

Q1: "What is a headcanon?"
A1: "A headcanon is a personal interpretation, belief, or idea about a fictional character, relationship, or universe that hasn't been officially confirmed by the creator. Fans use headcanons to add depth and explore 'what if' scenarios."

Q2: "How does the Headcanon Generator work?"
A2: "Our tool uses advanced randomization combined with curated story tropes. You simply input a character's name, their fandom, and choose a specific vibe or 'tone' (like Angst or Fluff). The engine instantly synthesizes fully unique narrative prompts for you to use."

Q3: "Is this tool completely free to use?"
A3: "Yes! All of our generators—including Character, Relationship, and Scenario headcanons—are 100% free with unlimited generation and no signup required."

Q4: "Can I use these headcanons for my fanfiction or roleplay?"
A4: "Absolutely! The generated ideas are designed specifically for writers, roleplayers, and creators to use as writing prompts, story starters, or character development exercises."

Q5: "Do you save my character inputs?"
A5: "No. Our tools run completely in your browser (client-side), meaning what you type is never sent to our servers or saved anywhere. Your privacy and creative ideas are yours alone."

--- BLOG ARTICLE FAQ (what-is-a-headcanon page + JSON-LD FAQPage schema) ---

Q1: "What is a headcanon in simple words?"
A1: "A headcanon is your personal idea about a character, relationship, or story world that is not officially confirmed in canon."

Q2: "Is headcanon the same as fanfiction?"
A2: "Not exactly. A headcanon is usually a concept or interpretation, while fanfiction is a full written story. A headcanon can become a fanfiction scene or plot."

Q3: "Can I use a headcanon generator for roleplay?"
A3: "Yes. A headcanon generator is useful for roleplay because it gives quick character hooks, emotional dynamics, and scene ideas you can use in sessions."

Additional FAQ (on-page only, no schema):
Q4: "Can a headcanon be wrong?"
A4: "It can conflict with canon, but it is not 'wrong' as a creative interpretation..."

Q5: "What makes a headcanon feel believable?"
A5: "Specific behavior + emotional reason + context. When all three are present, the headcanon feels natural and easy to write."

--- Article Body Text (key excerpts) ---

Definition: "A headcanon is a personal interpretation about a fictional character, relationship, or world that is not officially confirmed by the source material."

Fandom culture: "In fandom communities, a headcanon is how you interpret details that canon leaves open. Canon shows official facts... A headcanon starts where official information stops."

Canon vs headcanon vs fanfiction:
  • Canon: Official content from the original source.
  • Headcanon: Personal interpretation not officially confirmed.
  • Fanfiction: A complete story written by fans, often built using one or more headcanons.

5 Examples:
  1. Quiet character keeps a notebook of things they never say out loud
  2. Two rivals memorize each other's routines to make sure the other gets home safely
  3. Confident team leader secretly rehearses difficult conversations
  4. AU school pair that argues constantly defends each other against outsiders
  5. After a major event, character collects harmless routines to stay grounded

Writing Tips:
  1. Start with one clear behavior
  2. Add the emotional reason
  3. Place it in a scenario

Common Mistakes:
  • Too vague without scene-level behavior
  • Ignoring canon tone entirely
  • Piling too many traits into one headcanon
  • Writing concept notes but never testing in dialogue/action
